**Career Awareness Program**

**On March 30th, one SFS student delivered an enlightening career awareness talk to Forrest Hills High School students. The session centered around the vast array of opportunities within the cybersecurity field, emphasizing the critical need for highly skilled professionals. Additionally, valuable insights were shared regarding government-sponsored initiatives such as the SFS scholarship, highlighting the remarkable opportunities they offer.**

![Presenter at a recent Career Awareness Program](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Career-Awareness-Program.png)


On January 11th to 13th 2023, all of the SFS students attended the Scholarship For Service job fair. It was an enriching experience for all of them. They were able to participate in all the agency service information sessions to obtain more knowledge about various federal agencies. Moreover, they learned about all the cybersecurity job opportunities all the federal agencies are offering. Students were accompanied by Dr. Thaier Hayajneh (PI) and Dr. Gary Weiss (Co-PI) for the CyberCorps: Scholarship For Service program.

![CyberCorps Scholarship for Service Job Fair](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/CyberCorps_Scholarship_for_Service_Job_Fair.jpeg)


On December 10th, 2022, Fordham's Graduate School for Art and Science, with the conjunction between the computer science and cybersecurity masters program, offered an end-of-the-semester networking event for all students in both master programs. Five out of seven SFS students attended the event. They learned about current job opportunities in the private and public sectors. Moreover, they were able to network with current cybersecurity experts working in the field to learn more about their work perspective in the work area.

![Second Cybersecurity Networking Event with SFS Students](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Second_Cybersecurity_Networking_Event_with_SFS_Students.jpeg)


On November 16th, two of our seven SFS students attended the 2022 Aspen summit. They learned about various topics currently affecting the cybersecurity industry and new methods to protect our privacy. They were also able to interact with current DoD guest speakers who provided them with an overview of real job experience.

![2022 Aspen Cybersecurity Summit](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/2022_Aspen_Cybersecurity_Summit.jpeg)


On October 18, 2022, The Fordham ISACA student club hosted a networking event where students could meet different cybersecurity professionals and learn about job opportunities and the work they do. Six out of the seven students attend this event.
