# Fall 2022-Spring 2023 Activities

**Cyber 9/12 Competition**

**On September 12th, 2022, Students from the Cybersecurity Master’s Program participated in the 2022 NYC Cyber Strategy Challenge competition. **

![Cyber 9/12 Competition student participants](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Cyber-912-Competition.png)


**CTF Event**

**On September 30th, 2022, The Fordham Center for Cybersecurity student club conducted a CFT workshop. Students could see walkthroughs of example CTF problems and ask questions about them in an informal setting. Topics covered included Open-Source Intelligence, Cryptography, Password Cracking, Log Analysis, Scanning, Enumeration & Exploitation, Network Traffic Analysis, Forensics, Web Application Security, and Malware Analysis. **

![Students attending the 2022 CTF Event](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/CTF-Event-2022.png)


**NCL Workshop preparation**

**The Fordham Center For Cybersecurity student club prepared three mini-workshops to help students prepare for the National Cybersecurity League games. A total of 21 students participated in the 2022 NCL competition. **

![students preparing for NCL Workshop](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/NCL-Workshop-preparation.png)

**etworking Event**

**On October 18, 2022, The Fordham Center for Cybersecurity student club hosted a networking event where students could meet different cybersecurity professionals and learn about job opportunities and their work. Current faculty members of the Cybersecurity Master’s Program also attended this event, where they provided their expertise from an academic perspective. This event also included the participation of professionals from the private sector and the government sector. We had members of the DoD and DOJ attend this event. **

![Fall 2022 Networking Event](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Fall-2022-Networking-Event-.png)


**Spring 2023 Class Registration Event**

**On November 10th, 2022, the Fordham Center for Cybersecurity hosted a registration mixer event, where students obtained guidance on all the courses offered for the Cybersecurity M.S. spring 2023 semester. **

![Spring 2023 Class Registration Event](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Spring-2023-Class-Registration-Event.jpeg)


**The 2022 Aspen Cybersecurity Summit **

**On November 16th, students from the Cybersecurity Master’s program were invited to the 2022 Aspen Cyber summit. Students learned about various topics affecting the cybersecurity industry and new ways to protect our privacy. They also interacted with current DoD guest speakers who gave them an overview of actual job experience.**

![The 2022 Aspen Cybersecurity Summit](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/The-2022-Aspen-Cybersecurity-Summit.jpeg)


**Hands-on Cryptography and Bash Scripting Fundamentals workshop**

**On November 18th, 2022, The Fordham Center for Cybersecurity club conducted a hands-on cryptography and bash scripting workshop. The workshop includes cryptography topics, including encryption/decryption, key generation, key exchange, hashing, and message authentication. **

![Participants from the Hands-on Cryptography and Bash Scripting Fundamentals workshop](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Hands-on-Cryptography-and-Bash-Scripting-Fundamentals-workshop.png)


**Second Cybersecurity Networking Event**

**On December 10th, 2022, our Graduate School for Art and Science, with the conjunction between the computer science and cybersecurity master’s programs, offered an end-of-the-semester networking event for all students in both master programs. **

![Students attending the Second Cybersecurity Networking Event](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/center-for-cybersecurity/Second-Cybersecurity-Networking-Event.png)


### Spring 2023 First Networking Event

Students were able to learn about new career fairs for this semester, connect with other students in the program who are conducting reach, and learn about their possible areas of research opportunities.
