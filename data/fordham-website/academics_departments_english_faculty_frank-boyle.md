# Frank Boyle

![English Faculty Professor Frank Boyle](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/english/Frank-Boyle.png)

### Associate Professor

Director, Fordham College Honors Program (Lincoln Center)

**(On Leave Spring 2026)**

BA, Columbia University; Ph.D., Trinity College, Dublin

Research and Teaching Interests: Eighteenth-century satire; early-modern science; travel and travel theory

-
[Frank Boyle](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#593f2d3b3620353c193f362b3d313834773c3d2c)'s publications include(Stanford, 2000) and numerous articles on eighteenth-century subjects, including the essay on Swift in the recent[Swift As Nemesis: Modernity and its Satirist](https://books.google.com/books?id=oMoo2Fh86pMC&printsec=frontcover)*Blackwell Companion to Satire*. He is an editor of*Scriblerian*, and chairs Fordham's Eighteenth Century Study Group and the New York Eighteenth Century seminar. He finished a faculty fellowship in 2009 during which he drafted*Swift and the Brain*(working title), which was introduced to Fordham as the lecture "Pandemonium in the Dressing Room: Representation and the (Early) Modern Brain."
