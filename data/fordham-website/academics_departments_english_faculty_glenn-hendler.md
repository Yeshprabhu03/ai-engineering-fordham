# Glenn Hendler

![English Faculty Profile Picture](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/Hendler_240_1.jpg)

### Professor of English and American Studies

B.A., Brown University; Ph.D., Northwestern University

Research and Teaching Interests: Nineteenth-century American literature; cultural studies; critical theory; popular music; writing studies.

-
[Glenn Hendler](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#b6d1ded3d8d2dad3c4f6d0d9c4d2ded7db98d3d2c3)is the author of(North Carolina, 2001) and*Public Sentiments: Structures of Feeling in Nineteenth-Century American Literature*(Bloomsbury-33 1/3, 2020). He is also the co-editor of three books:*David Bowie’s Diamond Dogs*(California, 1999); an edition of Walt Whitman's temperance novel[Sentimental Men: Masculinity and the Politics of Affect in American Culture](https://www.ucpress.edu/books/sentimental-men/paper)(Duke, 2007) and*Franklin Evans; or, The Inebriate*(NYU, third edition 2020). Hendler’s most recent work includes an essay on “*Keywords for American Cultural Studies*[Walt Whitman and the Police](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-oxford-handbook-of-walt-whitman-9780192894847?cc=us&lang=en&)” and[further writings on David Bowie](https://www.routledge.com/One-Track-Mind-Capitalism-Technology-and-the-Art-of-the-Pop-Song/Siddiqi/p/book/9780367553722).
