# James Kim

![Prof. James Kim](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/James_Kim.jpg)


### Associate Professor

**(On Leave Spring 2026) **

B.A., University of Chicago; M.A., University of Virginia; Ph.D., University of Virginia

Research and Teaching Interests: Asian American literature and culture; eighteenth-century British literature and culture

-
[James Kim](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#bedcd4dfd5d7d3fed8d1ccdad6dfd390dbdacb)is Associate Professor of English and Comparative Literature. His scholarship has appeared in*Camera Obscura*,*The Eighteenth Century: Theory and Interpretation*,*Eighteenth-Century Fiction*,*MELUS*, and*Asian American Literature in Transition, 1965-1996*, edited by Asha Nadkarni and Cathy Schlund-Vials. Since 2016, he has co-directed the Seminar on American Studies at Columbia University. Before arriving at Fordham, he was Minority Scholar-in-Residence at Pomona College. In Fall 2018, he taught as a Visiting Associate Professor of American Studies at Princeton University.
