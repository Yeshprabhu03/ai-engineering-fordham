# John Bugg

![Prof. John Bugg](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/John_Bugg_1.jpg)

### Professor; Associate Chair, Rose Hill Undergraduate Studies

BA St. Mary's University; MA Fordham University; PhD Princeton University

British Romanticism; print culture and censorship; book history; legal and political history; empire and abolitionism; peace studies.

-
[John Bugg](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#751700121235131a07111d14185b101100)is author of(Stanford University Press, 2014) and*Five Long Winters: The Trials of British Romanticism*(Oxford University Press, 2022), and editor of*British Romanticism and Peace*(Oxford University Press, 2016), and Wuthering Heights (Oxford World's Classics, 2020). His essays and reviews have appeared in*The Joseph Johnson Letterbook**PMLA, ELH, Eighteenth-Century Studies, TLS, Romanticism, European Romantic Review*, and*Keats-Shelley Journal*. He has held fellowships from the Whiting Foundation, the Society of Fellows at Columbia University, and the National Endowment for the Humanities. He is co-founder, with Sarah Zimmerman, of the[Fordham Romanticism Group](http://www.fordhamromanticism.org/index.html). He is currently working on a cultural history of City Lights Books for Scribner.
