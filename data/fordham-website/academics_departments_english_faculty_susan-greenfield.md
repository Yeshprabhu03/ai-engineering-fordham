# Susan Greenfield

![English Faculty Professor Susan Greenfield](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/english/Susan-Greenfield.jpg)

## Professor

B.A., Brown University; A.M. and Ph.D., University of Pennsylvania

Research and Teaching Interests: Eighteenth-century novel (including Jane Austen); gender studies; early modern epistemology; psychoanalytic theory

-
[Susan Greenfield](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#8deaffe8e8e3ebe4e8e1e9cdebe2ffe9e5ece0a3e8e9f8)is the author of(Wayne State, 2002; paperback 2003), and co-editor of[Mothering Daughters: Novels and the Politics of Family Romance, Frances Burney to Jane Austen](https://books.google.com/books?id=3tvMLy_MSt8C&printsec=frontcover)(Kentucky, 1999). Her articles have appeared in numerous journals, including[Inventing Maternity: Politics, Science, and Literature, 1650-1865](http://www.kentuckypress.com/live/title_detail.php?titleid=1838)*Eighteenth-Century Studies*,*PMLA*, and*ELH*. Her current research focuses on perception, personhood, and property in the eighteenth- and early nineteenth-century novel.
