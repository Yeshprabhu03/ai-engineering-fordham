# History Faculty

Below the faculty members of the Department of History are listed. Many members of the History faculty at Fordham are also involved in the university's many fine [interdisciplinary programs](/academics/departments/history/about-us/interdisciplinary-programs/).

[Sal Acosta](/academics/departments/history/faculty/sal-acosta/) (2010), Associate Professor of History. Ph.D., University of Arizona. US Latino/a; Family; Gender; Popular Culture; Immigration and Ethnicity; Southwest US. *(Global)*

[Mattie Armstrong](/academics/departments/history/faculty/mattie-armstrong-price/) (2018), Assistant Professor of History. Ph.D., University of California, Berkeley. Modern Britain; Gender and Sexuality; Race and Empire; Global Labor History. *(Global)*

[Doron Ben-Atar](/academics/departments/history/faculty/doron-ben-atar/) (1996), Professor of History. Ph.D., Columbia. Revolutionary and early-national United States; early American foreign policy; psychohistory.

[Scott G. Bruce](/academics/departments/history/faculty/scott-g-bruce/) (2018), Professor of History. Ph.D., Princeton. Religion and culture, ca. 400-1200 CE; monasticism, hagiography, and reception of the classical tradition.

[Elizabeth Comuzzi](/academics/departments/history/faculty/elizabeth-comuzzi/) (2020), Assistant Professor of History, PhD, University of California, Los Angeles. Medieval social and economic history; women, family and demography; urbanization, labor and guilds; notarial culture; the Mediterranean; the Pyrenees; the Crown of Aragon/Catalonia

[Saul Cornell](/academics/departments/history/faculty/saul-cornell/) (2009) Paul and Diane Guenther Chair in American History. Ph.D., Pennsylvania. Legal/constitutional history, early American history, and American intellectual and cultural history.

[Christopher Dietrich](/academics/departments/history/faculty/christopher-dietrich/) (2012), Professor of History. Ph.D., University of Texas at Austin. 18th to 20th Century U.S. Foreign Relations; International History; Intellectual History; Political History.* (Global)*

[Claire Gherini](/academics/departments/history/faculty/claire-gherini/) (2016), Associate Professor of History. Ph.D., Johns Hopkins. British Atlantic World; Colonial North America and the Caribbean; History of Medicine; Women and Gender; Slavery and Emancipation; Human and Non-Human Animal Relationships. *(Global)*

[David Hamlin](/academics/departments/history/faculty/david-hamlin/) (2004), Professor of History. Ph.D., Brown. Modern Germany; cultural and economic history.

[Stephanie M. Huezo](/academics/departments/history/faculty/stephanie-huezo/) (2020), Assistant Professor of History. Ph.D., Indiana University,

Bloomington. Central America; Latin America; El Salvador; Salvadoran Diaspora; Immigrant

Movements; Popular Education; Oral History; Memory and Commemoration.

[Amir Idris](/academics/departments/history/faculty/amir-idris/) (2022), Professor of History. Ph.D., Queen’s University, Canada. Slavery, Colonialism, Race, Empires, States - Africa in global and transnational history.

[Samantha Iyer](/academics/departments/history/faculty/samantha-iyer/) (2016), Associate Professor of History. Ph.D., University of California, Berkeley. Modern United States, Modern Middle East, Modern South Asia, Political Economy, Environmental History, Agrarian History *(Global)*

[Christopher Maginn](/academics/departments/history/faculty/christopher-maginn/) (2004), Professor of History. Ph.D., National University of Ireland, Galway. Early modern Irish and British History; Gaelic Ireland; the Tudor state; British state formation.
