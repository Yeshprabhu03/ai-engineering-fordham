# Job Opportunities for Dissertators

**Careers for Historians**

[Careers for Students of History](https://www.historians.org/jobs-and-professional-development/career-resources/careers-for-students-of-history): an AHA publication that stresses the types of careers that those with a BA or MA in History are prepared to take on. You can order your own copy or use the one in the History Department.

Academic Jobs:

“[Applying for a Job at a Liberal Arts College](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2003/0309/0309pro1.cfm),” AHA Perspectives (2003)

“[Temping toward the Tenure Track](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2008/0812/0812gra2.cfm),” AHA Perspectives (2008)

“[From Teaching Assistant to Tenure-Track Faculty](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2008/0812/0812gra3.cfm)” AHA Perspectives (2008)

“[Going to a Community College](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2008/0812/0812gra4.cfm)” AHA Perspectives (2008)

See Applying for an Academic Position(no link)

Jobs outside Academia

[Aviso ](http://www.aam-us.org/)(The American Association of Museums)

[Society of American Archivists](http://www.archivists.org/), Employment Bulletin

[National Council on Public History](http://www.ncph.org/) lists archival employment opportunities

“[Practical Advice on Getting a Public History Job](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2006/0604/0604pro1.cfm),” AHA Perspectives (2006)

“[From Graduate School to Public History](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2008/0812/0812gra5.cfm),” AHA Perspectives (2008)

[Dispatch](http://www.aaslh.org/) (The American Association for State and Local History) publishes employment opportunities at historical societies

See also “Beyond Academe: [The Internet Gateway to Nonacademic Careers](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2005/0501/0501new4.cfm),” AHA Perspectives (2005)

**Federal, State, and Local Government Jobs**

[Office of Personnel Management ](https://www.opm.gov/)(Federal Government) lists jobs relating to historians, especially under “GS-170 Historian”

[Society for History in the Federal Government](http://www.h-net.org/~fedhist/) provides a listserv offering information on conferences and employment possibilities in federal history.

See also A Directory of Federal Historical Programs and Activities (2009).

See also its 1992 publication, Historical Programs in the Federal Government: A Guide.

“[Looking for a Federal Job? Some Practical Advice](http://www.historians.org/perspectives/issues/2006/0605/0605pro1.cfm),” AHA Perspectives (2006)

Local and State Government Jobs gives links to websites for local and state government jobs across the U.S.

“What Do Historians Do?” AHA Perspectives (1999)
