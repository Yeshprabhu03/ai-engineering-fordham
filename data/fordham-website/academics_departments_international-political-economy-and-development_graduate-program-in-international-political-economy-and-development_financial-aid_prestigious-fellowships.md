Applicants to Fordham University's Graduate Program in International Political Economy and Development (IPED) are urged to apply for prestigious fellowships awarded by various foundations, institutions, and governments.

The IPED Program matches outside prestigious fellowships when needed with an award of full tuition remission.

A number of international students in the past have had their studies at Fordham funded by the United States through its [Fulbright Program](http://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/). The [Organization of American States](http://www.oas.org/en/default.asp) has also funded many of our students from Latin America and the Caribbean.

You may wish to visit [Fastweb](http://www.fastweb.com/), a scholarship search engine that lets users search without cost a data base of over 180,000 private scholarships.

Current students in the IPED Program who wish to obtain post-graduate prestigious awards should contact Fordham's Office of Prestigious Awards (718-817-2771) for information about the [Fulbright Program](http://www.cies.org/), [Boren Awards](http://www.borenawards.org/) (also known as the National Security Education Program Fellowship), [Rotary International Foundation Scholarships](https://www.rotary.org/en), [United States Presidential Management Fellowship Program](https://www.pmf.gov/), [International Development Fellows Program](http://www.crs.org/about/careers/fellowships/), and opportunities sponsored by The Organization of American States, as well as other awards. IPED students have been very successful in winning these awards in the past.
