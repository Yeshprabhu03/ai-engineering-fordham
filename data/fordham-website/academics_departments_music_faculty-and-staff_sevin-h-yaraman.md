Dr. Yaraman, a music theorist, teaches Music for Dancers, and seminars on music and dance, and the perception of others. She was the recipient of an AAUW fellowship in support of her book

*Revolving Embrace: The Waltz, as Sex, Steps, and Sound*(2002) and received a fellowship from the Istanbul Tarihi Turk Muzigi Toplulugu (Istanbul Historical Turkish Music Society) for her research on the role of music and movement in the Sufi contemplative practices of Turkey. Recently, Yaraman received a Faculty Fellowship from Fordham to develop her current interest in Music and Disability. -
