# Psychology Department Faculty Achievements

[Lindsay Hoyt](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/lindsay-till-hoyt/) was awarded a grant through [Faculty-Led Initiatives at Fordham](/about/leadership-and-administration/administrative-offices/external-affairs/center-for-community-engaged-learning/engaged-faculty/community-building-and-professional-development/faculty-led-initiatives/academic-year-2023-4/) to co-lead the project "Youth-Led Participatory Action Research." This collaborative endeavor will examine youth's school climate and launch a new Network of support for local youth who want to advance the cause of allyship for LGBTQ+ students.

[Elizabeth Raposa](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/elizabeth-raposa/) was awarded funding to lead the project "Addressing Mental Health Needs in the Bronx with Third-Wave Cognitive Behavioral Therapies" through [Faculty-Led Initiatives at Fordham](/about/leadership-and-administration/administrative-offices/external-affairs/center-for-community-engaged-learning/engaged-faculty/community-building-and-professional-development/faculty-led-initiatives/academic-year-2023-4/). Dr. Raposa and her team will establish a collaboration between the Department of Psychology and one or more community organizations serving all Bronx residents (including [Fordham Community Mental Health Clinic](/academics/departments/psychology/graduate-program/phd-in-clinical-psychology/fordham-community-mental-health-clinic/)) to address unmet mental health needs, including common issues like depression, anxiety, and substance use, consistent with Fordham’s commitment to care for the whole person.

[Shellae Versey ](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/h-shellae-versey/)was awarded a grant (through [Faculty-Led Initiatives at Fordham](/about/leadership-and-administration/administrative-offices/external-affairs/center-for-community-engaged-learning/engaged-faculty/community-building-and-professional-development/faculty-led-initiatives/academic-year-2023-4/)) to co-lead a Community Innovation Lab (CIL), which will examine large-scale policy and community-led initiatives to address the effects of climate change on urban food systems.

[Alexander Kriss](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/alexander-kriss/) was awarded the CCEL Faculty-Led Initiative Grant to launch the Community Psychoanalysis Project within the [Fordham Community Mental Health Clinic ](/academics/departments/psychology/graduate-program/phd-in-clinical-psychology/fordham-community-mental-health-clinic/)(FCMHC).

[Andrew Rasmussen](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/andrew-rasmussen/) was awarded a grant from Fordham University Office of Research and the Center for Community Engaged Learning to work with the community-based organization [Union Fouta](https://www.unionfouta.org/ ) in a project to address cardiovascular health.

[Barry Rosenfeld](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/barry-rosenfeld/) served as the President of the [American Psychology-Law Society](https://ap-ls.org/ ) (Division 41 of the American Psychological Association).

[Celia Fisher](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/celia-b-fisher/) was awarded a grant from the [Greenwall Foundation](https://greenwall.org/ ) as Co-Principal Investigator, entitled "Who should be treated? The ethical challenges of administering opioid agonist treatment (OAT) for people who inject drugs during COVID-19". Dr. Fisher also served as a member of the Data Safety Monitoring Board for the NIDA Healing Communities Study and on two NIH HIV/AIDS scientific review panels.

[David Budescu](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/faculty-emeriti/david-budescu/) served as the editor of the APA journal [DECISION ](https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/dec)and became a [Jefferson Science Fellow]( https://sites.nationalacademies.org/PGA/Jefferson/PGA_375246) at USAID. Dr. Budesco also delivered a Distinguished Lecture titled "Harnessing the Wisdom of Crowds: Lessons about Judgmental Forecasting" at the National Academy of Sciences.

[Dean McKay](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/dean-mckay/) co-edited Springer's [Handbook of Child and Adolescent Anxiety Disorders](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-031-14080-8) and published several book chapters in different books, including the book he edited and in [Pseudoscience in Therapy](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/pseudoscience-in-therapy/B595605E19C0E8DAD7878ACC168E2225 ). Dr. McKay was also awarded a grant by the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) to work as a site PI on the project entitled "Trans-Ancestry Genomic Analysis of Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder" and served as a consultant on a grant also funded by NIMH.

[Elissa Aminoff](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/elissa-aminoff/) was awarded the Beacon Exemplar Award by the [United Student Government](https://usgrh.fordham.edu/ ) at Fordham University.

[Harold Takooshian](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/faculty-emeriti/harold-takooshian/#d.en.18372) was awarded the [Frances Mullen Award](https://icpweb.org/awards/frances-mullen-award/) for Distinguished Contributions to International Psychology and co-edited two books: [Global Mental, Spiritual, and Social Health](https://www.infoagepub.com/products/Behavioral-Science-in-the-Global-Arena-Mental-Spiritual-Social-Health) and [Global Health: Trends and Issues]( https://www.infoagepub.com/products/Behavioral-Science-in-the-Global-Arena-Health-Trends-and-Issues).

[Haruka Minami](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/haruka-minami/#d.en.18525) published in [Nicotine & Tobacco Research](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35271721/ ), the top journal in the field of nicotine and tobacco.

[Heining Cham](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/heining-cham/) was awarded the 2023 [Anne Anastasi Distinguished Early Career Contributions Award](https://www.apadivisions.org/division-5/awards/anastasi-early-career), which recognizes scholars who made outstanding contributions to quantitative research methods. Dr. Cham also became a fellow of the [American Psychological Association Division 5](https://www.apa.org/about/division/div5) (Quantitative and Qualitative Methods).

[Joshua Brown](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/joshua-l-brown/) was awarded an Interdisciplinary Research Grant through Fordham's Office of Research to co-lead the project "School-based Air Quality and Youth Mental Health and Academic Achievement in Two Bronx Schools" with Drs. Amy Roy (Psychology) and Stephen Holler (Physics). This collaborative endeavor is examining associations between classroom-level air quality indicators and youth behavior and academic performance with students in sixth through eight grades in two partnering middle schools in the Bronx.

[Keith Cruise](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/keith-cruise/) received funding under a competitive grant awarded to the University of Connecticut Health Center to continue his work as the Co-Director of the Center for Trauma Recovery and Juvenile Justice (CTRJJ). The Center brings together national leaders working with adolescents and families with justice system involvement, with the goal of informing a national framework for trauma-informed services to all youth and families in line with Fordham’s commitment to human dignity. Dr. Cruise was also awarded a grant to work as a Co-Principal Investigator at the [Mother Cabrini Health Foundation](https://cabrinihealth.org/ ) to enhance clinical mental health services to adolescents and families residing in the Bronx.

[Lindsay Hoyt](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/lindsay-till-hoyt/) was awarded a grant from the [National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute](https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/ ) to work as a Co-Principal Investigator on *The 3E Study: Economic and Educational Contributors to Emerging Adults’ Cardiometabolic Health*.

[Molly Zimmerman](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/molly-e-zimmerman/) was awarded a grant from the[ National Institutes of Health](https://www.nih.gov/) to work as a Co-Principal Investigator on the project *Heading and Soccer: Understanding Cognitive Risks, Benefits, and Potential Mediating Role of White Matter*.

[Monica Rivera Mindt](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/monica-rivera-mindt/) served as a Content Expert at the [Minnesota 2022 Conference to Update Education and Training Guidelines in Clinical Neuropsychology](https://minnesotaconference.org/ ). Dr. Mindt also delivered two keynote addresses focused on increasing access to neuroimaging studies (at the Annual Human Amyloid Imaging Conference in Miami) and dementia research and care (at the South Texas Alzheimer’s Conference). Dr. Mindt was also awarded new grants for several different projects, including Biomarker Evaluation in Young Onset Dementia from Diverse Populations (BEYONDD) and The Health & Aging Brain Study – Health Disparities (HABS-HD) (both funded by NIH/NIA), reflecting our department’s commitment to inclusion and care for the whole person.

[Selin Gülgöz](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/selin-gulgoz/) presented her research on gender development, cognition, and markers in children and youth at five different conferences, including three international conferences in Belgium, Ireland, and Canada. Dr. Gülgöz was also invited to speak at Princeton University's Department of Psychology.

[Shellae Versey](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/h-shellae-versey/) was awarded a Fordham Grant to promote the colloquium series and a reading group focused on moving theory into action, aligned with our commitment to justice. Dr. Versey also published chapters in two different books: [Beyond WEIRD: Psychobiography in Times of Transcultural and Transdisciplinary Perspectives](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-031-28827-2) and [A Research Agenda for Digital Geographies](https://figshare.le.ac.uk/articles/chapter/Chapter_7_Wearable_biosensors_an_agenda_for_digital_embodied_methods/23507427 ).

[Tiffany Yip](/academics/departments/psychology/faculty-and-staff/tiffany-yip/) received the [Society for Research on Adolescence](https://www.s-r-a.org/)'s Outstanding Mentor Award.
