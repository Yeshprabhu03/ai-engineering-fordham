**About**

Fadi Skeiker is a Professor of Theatre with over two decades of experience in academia and the arts. Fadi is celebrated for his innovative approach to theater, seamlessly integrating social justice themes and diasporic identities into his academic curriculum and professional practice.

Fadiâ€™s scholarly work focuses on the intersection of theater and social justice, exploring how performance can serve as a powerful tool for advocacy and change. He has directed numerous productions that highlight issues of migration, identity, and human rights, receiving critical acclaim for his thought-provoking and impactful work.

In addition to his teaching and directing, Fadi is an accomplished writer and editor, contributing extensively to academic journals and conferences. His research delves into the role of theater in diasporic communities, examining how the arts can provide a voice for marginalized groups and facilitate cross-cultural understanding.

Fadi Skeiker holds a PhD in Performance as Public Practice from The University of Texas at Austin and a graduate degree from Emerson College in Boston. He has held academic positions at The University of Jordan in Amman, Jordan; Free University of Berlin in Germany, The University of Minho in Portugal, and The University of the Arts in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania and has been a recipient of several prestigious grants and fellowships. His commitment to the arts and social justice continues to inspire students, practitioners, and audiences worldwide.
