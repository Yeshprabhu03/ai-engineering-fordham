# Manresa Program Application Information

![Two Female Students Sitting Outside](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/KGamble_102714_2448__SM.jpg)

Admission to the Manresa Scholars Program is competitive. If you would like to be considered for the Program, you must complete three (3) tasks:

- The 2025-2026 will open on Wednesday, May 7th and is due Monday, May 26, 2025. This Pre-Application requires submission of a 300-word essay (for topic, see bottom of this page).
- Complete the
[Office of Residential Life](/student-life/living-on-campus/living-on-campus-at-rose-hill/)'s general housing application, which will be made available on Thursday, May 29, 2025. Be certain to indicate[Loyola Hall](/student-life/living-on-campus/living-on-campus-at-rose-hill/residence-halls/first-year-student-housing/loyola-hall-residential-college/)(where the Program is housed) as one of your top two choices.

- If accepted, confirm your place in the Program by replying to your acceptance email, which would be sent to your Fordham email in early June.

Your admission to Manresa also brings along with it important responsibilities. Learn more about our [community standards](/fordham-college-at-rose-hill/academics/living-and-learning-communities/manresa-program/application-information/community-standards/) for you as a Manresa Scholar.

The newly-renovated

[Loyola Hall](/student-life/living-on-campus/living-on-campus-at-rose-hill/residence-halls/first-year-student-housing/loyola-hall-residential-college/)is the home of the Manresa Scholars Program. The Manresa Program is a rigorous and exciting integrated learning opportunity for freshmen at the Rose Hill campus.If you are a

**Fordham College at Rose Hill (FCRH)**student admitted to the Program, you will take a[Manresa seminar](/fordham-college-at-rose-hill/academics/living-and-learning-communities/manresa-program/course-offerings/)course from one of four options, taught by one of Fordham's most talented professor-scholars.. You get to know your professor and classmates well, creatively investigate academic disciplines in the Ignatian tradition, engage with the community around you, explore questions about the value and meaning of liberal arts education in the Jesuit tradition, and integrate in-class learning with[co-curricular activities](/fordham-college-at-rose-hill/academics/living-and-learning-communities/manresa-program/event-highlights/)that take advantage of Fordham's location in New York City and the intellectual curiosity of your peers.If you are a

**Gabelli School of Business (GSB)**student admitted to the Program, you will take one of[three-course](/fordham-college-at-rose-hill/academics/living-and-learning-communities/manresa-program/course-offerings/)[options](/fordham-college-at-rose-hill/academics/living-and-learning-communities/manresa-program/course-offerings/), one of which is a specialized version of "The Ground Floor." This course introduces you to the foundations of business, prompts you to work with peers to develop a business plan, challenges you to think "entrepreneurially," and exposes you to multinational corporations in New York City. -
