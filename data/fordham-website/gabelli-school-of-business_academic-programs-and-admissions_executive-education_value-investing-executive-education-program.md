### An Immersive Executive-Level Value Investing Course

As a participant in the 2 ½ day **Value Investing Executive Education Program**, you’ll be immersed in the principles and main discipline of **fundamental investing**, learning how to gain an edge in the stock market. The program will offer a deep understanding of the key drivers of a **stock's valuation**, explaining the critical role competitive advantage plays in its ability to create shareholder value. It will explore the tools that allow investors to assess investor expectations for future growth, and will utilize them throughout the course to evaluate five fascinating live case studies: **Apple • Nvidia • Amazon • Chipotle • Uber**

The investment case for AI and its potential on impacting the investment process also will be a focus of discussion. We will look at how it will impact the investment profession and where the biggest opportunities for investment lie.

### You’ll Walk Away With…

- A richer understanding of the basics of
**fundamental investing**and how to gain an edge in the stock market. - A deeper comprehension of the
**stock valuation**process, including the importance of correctly assessing a company’s competitive advantage. - Access to more sophisticated tools for valuing growth, as well as insights into possible AI investments and the potential impact of AI on the investment process.
- Networking opportunities that provide critical connections for professional growth, career advancement, and the exchange of new ideas.

### Paul Johnson - Instructor

![Paul Johnson](/media/home/schools/gabelli-school-of-business/Paul_Johnson_Square.jpg)


Paul Johnson has been an adjunct professor at Fordham University’s Gabelli School of Business since 2015. He teaches Value Investing and is a fellow at Fordham’s [Gabelli Center for Global Security Analysis](https://www.fordhamgabellicenter.org/). Johnson received the Gabelli School of Business graduate-level Dean’s Award for Faculty Excellence in May 2017 and the Adjunct Faculty Dean’s Award for Impact in May 2021. He also is an adjunct professor at Columbia Business School, where he has taught 58 semester-long courses on securities analysis and value investing to more than 3,500 full-time and Executive M.B.A. students.
