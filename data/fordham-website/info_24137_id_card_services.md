# Fordham ID

**For Students, Faculty, Staff**

## Overview

Your Fordham ID functions as your official University identification, but it is more than just an identification card – it’s an essential part of campus life at Fordham University. Your ID is used for:

**Building Access**to residence halls, offices,[fitness centers](https://www.fordham.edu/student-life/safety-health-and-wellness/fitness-and-recreation/fitness-and-recreation-centers/), certain computer labs,[Fordham libraries](https://www.fordham.edu/resources/libraries/about-us/), and other secured areas on campus.services around campus.**Ram Print**- Your
**meal plan**and DCB (Declining Balance) at campus dining locations. **Student discounts**at NYC museums, theaters, and some local shops. Contact the Office of Student Leadership and Community Development for more info.

We currently offer two types of ID cards, but are moving towards an eID-first model:

**Electronic ID (eID) card:**Accessible via the Fordham app on your mobile device. This will be the primary ID for all incoming students in Fall 2025.**Physical ID card:**Hard plastic ID card. Current students, faculty, and staff who opted for a physical card can continue to use it.

-
New employees receive specific instructions on how to obtain their ID cards from Human Resources during the hiring process.

### Set up your eID

**Download the Fordham Mobile Go App**to your phone from the[App Store for iOS](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fordham-university-mobile-go-app/id1111290022)or[Google Play for Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=edu.fordham.mobile&hl=en_US)- Open the app and click
**Menu**(bottom right corner) - Scroll down to
**Additional Resources section**and click**Fordham ID Setup** - Sign in with your Fordham credentials
- Click
**Upload Your Photo**and follow the instructions to upload the photo that will appear on your eID - After uploading, click
**Electronic ID Opt-in/Out** - Choose
**Electronic eID**, scroll down, and click**Submit**. - You’ll see an
**activation code**—copy it. - Go back to the app home page and click
**eID Campus Access**. - Paste the code you copied to activate your mobile ID.

### How to use your eID on Campus

- Unlock your mobile device and open the Fordham Mobile Go App.
- Hold the back of your device up to the ID reader for 10 seconds.

Important Notes:

- Your mobile device needs to be unlocked with the app running in order to tap.
- You do NOT need to log in with MFA or display your eID for the tap to work.
- Your eWallet will pop up when tapping, but your information is not being read.
- Ensure your mobile device and Fordham Mobile Go App are enabled with Bluetooth and location services. Android devices will also need NFC enabled.
- We recommend keeping the app always running in the background to easily tap

### Physical ID Cards

While we highly recommend the eID for its ease of use and security features, we understand that there may be circumstances where a physical ID card will be necessary.

- Hold your physical ID card near a scanner for a few seconds to use it.
- If you currently have a physical ID card, you can still continue to use it around campus.
- Keep it with you at all times & guard your card as you would any important document. Do not lend your card to anyone for any reason.
- Card replacement: If you lose your physical ID card, the cost to replace it is
**$30**. Visit a Physical ID Service Center to replace your card (see below). - If you would like to switch to an eID, follow the setup instructions above.

#### Important Update: Fall 2025 Transition to an eID-First Campus for Incoming Students

Fordham University is excited to announce a move towards a more convenient and sustainable campus experience! Beginning in the Fall of 2025, Fordham will become an

**electronic ID (eID) first campus**for incoming students. What this means for you:-
**First year and transfer students:**You will utilize an electronic ID accessible on your mobile device. -
**Current students:**You can continue to use your existing physical or electronic ID.
