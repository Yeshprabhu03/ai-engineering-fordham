# Learning Spaces Intern

**For Students**

**Department**: Teaching and Learning Environment (TLE)

**Position**: Learning Spaces Intern | RH or LC

**Supervisor**: Manager, Teaching and Learning Environment

**Summary**

Teaching and Learning Environment is looking for a bright, hard working student to perform daily checks of IT equipment in academic spaces. Candidate will be expected to check all academic spaces on a rotating schedule to test functionality of technology. Candidate should have working knowledge of audio and visual technologies, typical business computer hardware, Windows and Mac OSes, and IP networking; some training will be provided.

**Qualifications**

- Must be a registered Fordham student in good academic standing during the semester of their employment.
- Reliable, quick learner who can work with minimal supervision.
- Must have 15-19 hours per week available, primarily evenings and/or weekends.
- Must uphold, enforce and abide by all university policies.

**Responsibilities**

- Assess state of classroom technology, troubleshoot and repair common issues when discovered.
- Create tickets to document issues and repairs.
- Perform A/V device adjustments, firmware upgrades, and installations as directed by TLE Manager.
- Installation of software on Windows PCs and Macs.
- Gathering of new inventory data, confirmation of existing inventory data.
- Troubleshooting of A/V device networking, PC/Mac networking.
- Working together with TLE Manager to remotely troubleshoot A/V and computer issues.
- Performing other duties as required.

**Technical Knowledge**

- Computer hardware and software troubleshooting.
- Working knowledge of Windows 7, 10, OS X/macOS, TCP/IP.
- Familiarity with A/V standards, serial communications, and signal flow a plus.


**Compensation**: $18.00 per hour
