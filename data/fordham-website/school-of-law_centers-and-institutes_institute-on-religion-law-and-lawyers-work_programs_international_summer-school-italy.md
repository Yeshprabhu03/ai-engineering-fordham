# Weaving Dialogue: Fraternal Cities for Peace

**sustainable development**through the lens of

**Integral Ecology**.


This **Summer School** aims to create an interdisciplinary research and discussion space, fostering the analysis and reimagining of contemporary cities as places of peace, inclusion, and sustainability. With a scientific and applied approach, participants will engage in an intensive training program, guided by internationally recognized experts, featuring:

- Lectures
- Workshops
- Project-based activities
- Meetings with distinguished speakers and practitioners


This unique experience will provide participants with the tools and insights necessary to develop **innovative solutions for a more sustainable and inclusive urban future**.


- Philosophy & Complex Thought
- Rights, New Technologies & Sustainability
- Innovation, Management & Marketing
- Geopolitics & International Relations


Program Overview


The program is open to a maximum of 50 young participants (**ages** **18-30**) with a strong interest in **Integral Ecology and sustainable development**.

- Selection through a motivational interview
- Sections in Italian & English (translation available)

![Fordha Law Students in circle](/media/home/schools/school-of-law/SUMMER-SCHOOL-IN-ITALY.jpg)

Ways to Participate

There are **two ways to participate** in the Summer School:

**1. Attend as a participant**, engaging in the full training program.

**2. Attend and submit a paper or project idea**, which will be evaluated for presentation during the Summer School and considered for publication.

For those whose project ideas are selected, there will be an opportunity to continue developing their work under the guidance of tutors and faculty, fostering the practical application of the knowledge acquired.

Scholarships

Thanks to the support of partners and donors, each selected participant will receive a **scholarship worth €1,200**, covering the full cost of the training program.

**Attendees are responsible for their own travel expenses**.

Travel Expense Reimbursement Opportunity

International students may be eligible for travel expense reimbursement, depending on the availability of funds provided by the project's sponsors. Reimbursement opportunities will be discussed during the admission interview.

Participants will also have the optional opportunity to attend the Euro-Mediterranean Meeting on Integral Ecology** – **in **November 2025 in Taranto, Italy**.

This annual scientific gathering fosters dialogue between academia, institutions, and local communities on sustainable development and Mediterranean experiences in Integral Ecology.

**Virtual participation will also be available** for those who cannot attend in person.

Frequently Asked Questions:**Can I submit more than one work?**

Yes, up to two works per participant, each with a maximum of three co-authors.**Are travel expenses included?**

No, travel expenses are not included. The organizers offer scholarships covering the training program and room and board, as specified in the separate call. Participants are encouraged to seek financial support for travel from their home institutions.**Is physical attendance mandatory?**

Yes, the Summer School will take place in person in Castel Gandolfo from July 24 to 30, 2025.
