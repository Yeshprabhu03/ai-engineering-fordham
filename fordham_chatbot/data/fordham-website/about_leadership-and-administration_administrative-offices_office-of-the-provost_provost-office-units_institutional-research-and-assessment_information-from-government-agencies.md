# Information from Government Agencies

## Department of Homeland Security (DHS) STEM Designated CIP codes (for International Students)

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) graduates who attended Fordham on an **F-1 visa** may be eligible for a 24-month extension of post-graduation Optional Practical Training. Program CIP codes are provided in the [Bulletin](https://bulletin.fordham.edu/). Consult [the DHS STEM Designated program list](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/eligible-cip-codes-for-the-stem-opt-extension) to determine whether your major or program has been designated STEM. If your program has been included in the DHS list, you can learn more about application requirements and procedures from the [Office of International Services](/academics/academic-resources/international-services/students/post-graduation/stem-extension/).

## Major Fields of Study and Occupational Opportunities for All Students

The [National Center for Educational Statistics](https://nces.ed.gov/) partners with the [Bureau of Labor Statistics](https://www.bls.gov/) to identify the occupations commonly associated with particular college majors. Learn more about common career paths your Fordham education can lead you to using the “[Field of Degree](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/field-of-degree/home.htm)” option. Or use the [Occupational Outlook Handbook](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/) to learn what educational programs can prepare you for particular careers.

## National Center for Educational Statistics

Researchers and decision-makers can tap the data collected by the [National Center for Educational Statistics](https://nces.ed.gov/) (NCES) for useful metrics. Obtain data about Fordham or about any institution or set of institutions from this public website. Learn more from NCES guides or [jump into data selection](https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/InstitutionByName.aspx?goToReportId=0&sid=6fb35e44-6b0f-435d-bb3f-0324985efab3&rtid=0).
