# M.S in Data Science and Quantitative Economics Learning Goals

The master's in data science and quantitative economics program enables students to attain, by the time of graduation:

- A background in economic theory through instruction in
[ECON 6010](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=ECON%206010)Microeconomic Theory I and[ECON 6020](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=ECON%206020)Macroeconomic Theory I. - Updated skills in math and statistics through
[CISC 5450](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=CISC%205450)Mathematics for Data Science or[ECON 5710](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=ECON%205710)Mathematical Analysis in Economics, and through[ECON 6910](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=ECON%206910)Applied Econometrics or[ECON 6950](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=ECON%206950)Financial Econometrics. - Advanced technical skills to build and assess computational models and apply data science concepts and methods to economics data through
[CISC 5790](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=CISC%205790)Data Mining and[CISC 5800](https://nextbulletin.fordham.edu/search/?P=CISC%205800)Machine Learning. - An understanding of the theoretical framework of big data processing and hands-on experience in big data analytics and its applications.
- An ability to effectively communicate data science-related information to any audience and transform findings into actionable solutions to real-world problems.
