# Anne Fernald

### Professor

B.A., Wellesley College; M.A. and Ph.D., Yale University

Research and Teaching Interests: Composition and rhetoric; the essay; British modernism; twentieth-century anglophone literature; feminist literature, criticism, and theory; Virginia Woolf

-
[Anne E. Fernald](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#c9afacbba7a8a5ad89afa6bbada1a8a4e7acadbc)is Professor of English and Women’s, Gender, and Sexuality Studies at Fordham University. She is the author of(2006) as well as articles and reviews on Woolf and feminist modernism. She is the editor of the Cambridge University Press[Virginia Woolf: Feminism and the Reader](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1057/9780230600874)(2014) the[Mrs. Dalloway](https://www.cambridge.org/us/universitypress/subjects/literature/english-literature-1900-1945/mrs-dalloway?format=HB)(2021), and the[Norton Critical Edition of Mrs. Dalloway](https://wwnorton.com/books/9780393655995)(2021). She is one of the editors of[Oxford Handbook of Virginia Woolf](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-oxford-handbook-of-virginia-woolf-9780198811589?cc=us&lang=en&)*The Norton Reader*, a widely used anthology of essays. She was co-editor of*Modernism/modernity*2019-23. From 2004-15 she served as the director of first-year writing at Lincoln Center and served as Special Advisor to the Provost for Faculty Development from 2018-23. In that capacity, she served as the inaugural faculty co-chair for the university’s diversity council, and she co-led the university’s support for teachers during the COVID-19 lockdown. She is currently at work on a collective biography of eight modernist women writers.
