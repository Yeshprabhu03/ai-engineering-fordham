# Catherine Chaput

![Catherine Chaput](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/english/Catherine_Chaput.jpeg)

### Professor and Thomas J. Kavanagh Chair in Rhetoric

BA, Creighton University; MA, Binghamton University; PhD, University of Arizona

**Research and Teaching Interests**: rhetorical history, theory, and criticism with an emphasis on the role of affect and embodiment in public deliberation and structural inequality

-
[Catherine Chaput](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#395a5a5158494c4d795f564b5d515854175c5d4c)is the author ofand*Inside the Teaching Machine*and editor of*Market Affect and the Rhetoric of Political Economic Debates*and*Entertaining Fear: Rhetoric and the Political Economy of Social Control**Into the Gateway: Project on Power, Place, and Publics**.*She has guest edited several journal issues and written dozens of articles and book chapters on the role of rhetoric and writing in the maintenance and transformation of social, political economic, and institutional structures. Currently, she is interested in learning from contemporary Black Studies scholarship to rethink rhetorical approaches to public deliberation and social movement change
