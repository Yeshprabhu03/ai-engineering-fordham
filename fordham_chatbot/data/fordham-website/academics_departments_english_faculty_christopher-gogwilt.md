# Christopher GoGwilt

![English Faculty Profile Picture](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/GoGwilt_240_1.jpg)

### Professor

BA, Swarthmore; Ph.D., Princeton University

Research and Teaching Interests: Victorian and modernist studies (British, American, European, and comparative colonial and postcolonial literatures); postcolonial studies; ecology and literature; critical theory

-
[Christopher GoGwilt](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#1b7c747c6c72776f5b7d74697f737a76357e7f6e)is the author of(Oxford, 2011) which won the*The Passage of Literature: Genealogies of Modernism in Conrad, Rhys, and Pramoedya*[Modernist Studies Association book prize](https://msa.press.jhu.edu/prize/archive.html)for 2012. His most recent book is(forthcoming from Fordham UP, 2023). He is also the author of*The K-Effect: Romanization, Modernism, and the Spacing and Timing of Print Culture*(Stanford, 2000) and*The Fiction of Geopolitics: Afterimages of Culture from Wilkie Collins to Alfred Hitchcock**The Invention of the West: Joseph Conrad and the Double-Mapping of Europe and Empire*(Stanford, 1995). He has published numerous essays in the areas of Victorian studies, modernism, colonialism, and post-colonialism as book-chapters and in such journals as*Comparative Literature Studies*,*Cultural Critique*,*Modernism/Modernity*,*Mosaic*,*New German Critique*,*Victorian Studies*, and*The Yale Journal of Criticism*. Recent publications include “Jean Rhys and Indonesia: A Lineage and Alienage” (in, Bloomsbury, 2021)*Transnational Jean Rhys*[“Indonesian Dissidence and Modern Narrative Form: Pramoedya Ananta Toer”](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/9781118635193.ctwl0254)(in*A Companion to World Literature*, Wiley and Sons, 2019), and["The Novel and the Nation"](https://books.google.co.uk/books?hl=en&lr=&id=nPcRCgAAQBAJ&oi=fnd&pg=PA441&dq=gogwilt&ots=SW0cDKB0LM&sig=V4P-oOwkjg1ZeqeSoXx34QbPkIc#v=onepage&q=gogwilt&f=false)(in*A Companion to the English Novel.*Wiley-Blackwell, 2015). He is the co-editor ofde Gruyter, 2022) and*Westernness: Critical Reflections on the Spatio-Temporal Construction of the West*((Fordham University Press, 2018). His website is*Mocking Bird Technologies: The Poetics of Parroting, Mimicry, and Other Starling Tropes*[chris.gogwilt.com](http://chris.gogwilt.com/).
