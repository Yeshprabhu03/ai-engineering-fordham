# Thomas Hertweck

![](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/english/Thomas-Hertwerk.jpeg)


### Advanced Lecturer

BA, MA, Purdue University; PhD, University of Nevada, Reno

Research and Teaching Interests: Ecocriticism/environmental studies, food studies, 20th and 21st Century American literature and culture, film

-
Tom Hertweck is the editor of

(Rowman & Littlefield, 2014) and founding co-editor of the book series*Food on Film: Bringing Something New to the Table*with the University of Nevada Press. He is author of research focusing on ecocriticism and environmentality, food studies, vegan studies, and American literature and film. His current manuscript is a study of food commodity paratext located at the intersection of public reading practices and commercial food packaging, provisionally entitled “Narredibility: Reading and Eating in Postwar America,” so going to the grocery store with him becomes and hours-long adventure. Additionally, he is the most recent past-president and current vice-president of*Cultural Ecologies of Food*[the Kurt Vonnegut Society](https://blogs.charleston.edu/vonnegut/), the international scholarly organization devoted to the author’s work. Tom is also affiliated faculty in Fordham’s[Department of American Studies](https://www.fordham.edu/academics/departments/american-studies/).
