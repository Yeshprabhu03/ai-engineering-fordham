Carey Kasten is professor of Spanish language and literature at Fordham University. She researches contemporary Spanish culture and Spanish-speaking communities in New York City. She is the author of

(Fordham, 2024) and[Mutuality in El Barrio: Stories of the Little Sisters of the Assumption Family Health Service](https://www.fordhampress.com/9781531506438/mutuality-in-el-barrio/)*The Cultural Politics*(Bucknell, 2012). She also edited a special issue of the Bulletin of Spanish Studies on "The Baroque in the Construction of a National Culture in Francoist Spain." Dr. Kasten co-founded Fordham's "Initiative on Migrants, Migration and Human Dignity" and works to bring students and faculty to the Arizona- Mexico border to learn about the complex realities of migration. In 2021, she curated “[of Twentieth- Century Spanish Theater: Representing the Auto Sacramental](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/cultural-politics-of-twentiethcentury-spanish-theater-9781611483819/)[Hostile Terrain 94](https://fordhamuniversitygalleries.com/section/506193-HOSTILE%20TERRAIN%2094.html),” an art installation that depicts the loss of migrant life in the Sonoran desert, at Fordham University’s Lipani Gallery.
