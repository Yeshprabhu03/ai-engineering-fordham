**Sun Im Cho, Classic Piano**

DMA, The Graduate Center, CUNY

MM and Professional Studies, Manhattan School of MusicPianist Sun Im Cho is a prize winner of the Ibla Grand Prize International Music Competition in Italy and New York’s Artists International Competition. The

*Journal of Music*and*The Piano*applauded her "brilliant technique, thoughtful playing, and sensitive, colorful touch."Dr. Cho has appeared as a soloist at Carnegie Hall, Merkin Hall, Steinway Hall, Yamaha Hall, Lefrak Hall, Seoul Arts Center, and Sejong Cultural Center. She has had numerous piano concerto performances with the Metro Chamber Orchestra, the Manhattan Chamber Orchestra, the Korea Soloist Orchestra, and chamber music performances with the Lucidus Ensemble. She has given New York premiere performances of works by Nancy Van de Vate, Julien Yu, Andreas Tarkamnn, I-Chun Lee, and Tsang-Houei Hsu, to name a few.

Dr. Cho is a member of the National Education Association, the Associated Music Teachers League, and the American Musicological Society—Greater New York Chapter. She has been a judge for the LISMA International Music Competition, the Manhattan International Music Competition, the International Music Concours, the National Guild of Piano Teachers Audition, the Suffolk Piano Teachers Foundation Piano Competition. She is currently a faculty member at Fordham University, Westchester Community College (SUNY), and the ECPSM at Queen College.

[Dr. Andy Bhasin](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#7410161c15071d1a34121b06101c15195a111001), Director of Orchestral EnsemblesAndy Bhasin leads the Fordham University Symphony Orchestra and the Lincoln Center Chamber Orchestra. Dr. Bhasin has performed internationally in orchestral concerts with prominent ensembles such as the New World Symphony and at festivals including Spoleto (USA) and Music Academy of the West. He has had prominent recital appearances in such venues as the Gasteig in Munich, Germany, Merkin Concert Hall and Carnegie Hall in New York City as a prizewinner of the American Protege International Concerto Competition. His playing has been featured in Daniel Nearing’s 2016 feature film, Hogtown (named to the top ten films of 2016 by Ben Kenigsberg). He has worked as assistant conductor of the Stony Brook University Opera and frequently guest conducts the Brooklyn Symphony Orchestra. Dr. Bhasin holds a BM from New York University, an MM from the Hartt School, and a DMA from SUNY Stony Brook.

[Matthew Buttermann](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#731e1106070716011e121d1d33151c01171b121e5d161706), Director of Jazz Ensembles and Jazz OrchestraMatt Buttermann is a musician and educator in the New York area. He has served as the Assistant Director of Education at Jazz at Lincoln Center to develop instructional programs for youth for over 15 years as well as the director of Jazz Performance at Fordham University for the past 10 years. As an educator, programs Matt designed and taught have served thousands of students and garnered numerous awards, performances, and creative institutional partnerships. As a guitarist, Matt has performed with renowned artists including Clark Terry, Wynton Marsalis, Jimmy Heath, Marquis Hill, Herlin Riley, Wycliffe Gordon, Mulgrew Miller, Freddy Cole, Matt Wilson, Phil woods, and Ted Nash. Mr. Buttermann received his BFA in jazz studies from The City College of New York, MM from William Paterson University, and Ed.D from Teachers College, Columbia University.
