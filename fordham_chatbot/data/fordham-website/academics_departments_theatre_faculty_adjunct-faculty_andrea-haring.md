**About**

A director, actress and vocal coach, Andrea Haring is the Executive Director of The Linklater Center for Voice and Language and a Coordinator for Linklater Teacher Training. In addition to teaching at Fordham, she is currently on faculty at The New School MFA and BFA Acting programs . Andrea has previously taught at Yale School of Drama, Columbia University MFA Acting, and NYU Tisch Undergraduate Drama. Her teaching has included theater schools and companies in Ireland, Spain, Italy, Germany, the UK, Mexico and Iceland. She is a member of Labyrinth Theater Company and a Founding Member of Shakespeare and Company in Lenox, MA.

Andrea coaches extensively On and Off Broadway – including AMERICAN BUFFALO (Sam Rockwell, Lawrence Fishburne, Darren Criss), WALKING WITH GHOSTS (Gabriel Byrne), FOOL FOR LOVE (Sam Rockwell), WOLF HALL (RSC), DISGRACED (Ayad Aktar), THE MOTHERF****ER WITH THE HAT (Stephen Adly Guirgis, Bobby Canavale, Elizabeth Rodriguez). Off-Broadway and other coaching: DANNY AND THE DEEP BLUE SEA (Aubrey Plaza, Christopher Abbott), HALFWAY BITCHES GO STRAIGHT TO HEAVEN (Stephen Adly Guirgas, Atlantic), FOSSE/VERDON (Sam Rockwell), IN THE BLOOD (Russell G Jones, Signature), LIPS TOGETHER, TEETH APART (America Ferrera, Second Stage), JOHN GABRIEL BORKMAN (Alan Rickman, BAM).

In her corporate work, she’s taught voice and public speaking for leadership workshops to The Obama Foundation Scholars, The World Economic Forum Global Leadership Fellows, The Social Impact Fellowship Program, Columbia Business School Senior Executive Program, Senior Non-Profit Leaders Program, and to professionals of all disciplines who want freer, more expressive voices.
