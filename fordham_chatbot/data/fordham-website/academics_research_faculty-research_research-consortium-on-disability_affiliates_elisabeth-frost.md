# Elisabeth Frost

![Elisabeth Frost](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/Screen_Shot_2020_06_12_at_11.34.10_AM.png)

**Bio:**

A poet and scholar, [Elisabeth Frost](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#ec898a9e839f98ac8a839e88848d81c2898899) is the author of a collection of poetry, *All of Us* (White Pine Press, 2011); two chapbooks,* Rumor *(Mermaid Tenement Press, 2009) and *A Theory of the Vowel* (Red Glass Books, 2013); and a critical study, *The Feminist Avant-Garde in American Poetry** *(University of Iowa Press, 2003). She is also co-editor of [ Innovative Women Poets: An Anthology of Contemporary Poetry and Interviews ](https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=&ved=2ahUKEwjR_q_ej_mOAxUTlokEHdaFFSoQFnoECBUQAQ&url=https%3A%2F%2Fuipress.uiowa.edu%2Fbooks%2Finnovative-women-poets&usg=AOvVaw1nqjZ0dUYmZ64dD6Hu00RV&opi=89978449)(University of Iowa Press, 2006). Her text-image collaborations with the visual artist Dianne Kornberg have been shown at the Frost directed the Poets Out Loud reading series for a decade, and she founded and continues to edit the Poets Out Loud book series from Fordham University Press. She teaches disability studies, contemporary poetry, creative writing, and women's studies at the Lincoln Center campus, as well as in the graduate program at Rose Hill. Her website is

[elisabethfrost.net](http://www.elisabethfrost.net/).
