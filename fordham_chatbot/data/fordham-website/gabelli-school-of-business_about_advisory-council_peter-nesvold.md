Outside of work, Nesvold is an adjunct professor of corporate finance at Fordham and a fellow at the Gabelli Center for Global Security Analysis, and has published four books on M&A with McGraw-Hill. He currently serves on the boards of VestGen Wealth Partners (a $5 billion wealth management firm), SignatureFD (a $9 billion wealth management firm), and the Hampton Jitney, and previously served on the board of the CFA Society New York.

Nesvold graduated from the University of Pennsylvania (B.A., Economics with a concentration equivalent in accounting from The Wharton School) and Fordham Law (cum laude, Order of the Coif), where he was elected editor-in-chief of his law journal. He was previously a competitive long-distance runner, completing 19 full-marathons.
