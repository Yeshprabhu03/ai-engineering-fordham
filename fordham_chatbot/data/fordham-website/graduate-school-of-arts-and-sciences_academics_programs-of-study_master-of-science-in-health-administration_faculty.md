**Julia Kolak, Ph.D., Adjunct Professor, M.S. in Health Administration**Julia Kolak is a bioethicist and philosopher of medicine whose work focuses on the conceptual foundations of psychiatric classification and their implications for clinical decision-making. Her current research examines the normative underpinnings of psychiatric nosology, particularly the adoption of operational definitions in the DSM, and more broadly explores how notions of dysfunction, intervention, and health shape ethical frameworks in medicine. She also conducts research in healthcare ethics consultation methodology, reproductive ethics, experimental bioethics, research ethics, and the ethics of psychedelic medicine. In addition to her scholarship, she serves as Co-Chair of the Reproductive Ethics Affinity Group of the American Society for Bioethics and the Humanities (ASBH).

Dr. Kolak is currently a Research Associate at The Hastings Center for Bioethics, where she is engaged in NIH-funded projects on the ethical use of artificial intelligence in cell mapping and voice technologies. She was previously a Visiting Fellow at the Edmond & Lily Safra Center for Ethics at Harvard University and completed her clinical ethics fellowship at the Montefiore Einstein Center for Bioethics, where she also served on the ethics consultation service performing bedside mediation. She has taught bioethics extensively at the graduate and medical school levels and received the Leo M. Davidoff Society Award for excellence in medical education while at Albert Einstein College of Medicine. She earned her PhD in philosophy from The Graduate Center of the City University of New York, which was supported by fellowships from The Graduate Center, CUNY, and the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai where she was the former Senior Clinical Ethics Fellow. **Elby M. Nash, Ph.D., Adjunct Professor, M.S. in Health Administration**

Professor Nash is a senior practitioner, management consultant, executive educator, and technology innovation leader across the life sciences and healthcare industries for over 30 years. He has worked closely with executive and patient-facing stakeholders in world-class organizations on the development of digital health strategies focused on improved medical outcomes and reduced operating costs. As a successful entrepreneur and innovator, he pioneered the development and commercial deployment of integrated mobile solutions (“mHealth) for patient chronic disease management in multiple disease domains. As an educator, Dr. Nash is the co-author of a graduate textbook on managing IT organizations and was an associate professor at Stevens Institute of Technology’s Wesley J. Howe School of Technology Management for over a decade where he designed, developed, and delivered multiple graduate-level courses in healthcare and life sciences, IT strategy and the management of IT organizations to executives, practitioners, and administrators of healthcare and life sciences firms.[linkedin.com/in/elby-nash-006200277/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/elby-nash-006200277/)
