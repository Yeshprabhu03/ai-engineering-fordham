# Teaching Students with Disabilities (All Grades)

![Male professor lecturing in front of classroom](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/Male_professor_lecturing_in_front_of_classroom.jpg)

To help students with disabilities reach their personal and academic potential, it is essential to have skilled teachers who are well-versed in student learning and development, Pre K-12 curriculum content, and effective pedagogy. These teachers should also possess the knowledge and skills to adapt learning environments and experiences to meet the unique needs of each child.

The Special Education program for Grades PreK-12 prepares such educators through a rigorous academic curriculum. This program integrates a strong foundation in PreK-12 education and psychology with a comprehensive sequence of field experiences and student teaching opportunities, ensuring graduates are well-prepared to support diverse learners effectively.

## General Teacher Education Program Admissions Requirements

- 3.0 GPA
- Online application
- Official transcript(s)
- Resume or Curriculum Vitae (C.V.)
- Personal statement
- Two letters of recommendation

In addition to the general admissions requirements above, international applicants must have all transcripts translated into English and evaluated for credentialing in the United States by an accredited agency (World Education Services).

[Browse the Admissions section for more about the application and financial aid processes](/graduate-school-of-education/admissions/) and [review a list of courses](https://bulletin.fordham.edu/gse/initial-teaching-mst/tesol/#requirementstext).

## Fieldwork Requirements

A semester-long student teaching is required. In the fall or spring semester, students are in classrooms for five full days per week for 15 weeks.
