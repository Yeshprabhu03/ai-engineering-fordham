# RBERN Virtual Instructional Resources for Parents and Families

This list of resources was created to support bilingual parents and families for virtual learning opportunities.

## Websites

### Early Childhood (Ages 1 - 6)

[Beanstalk](https://beanstalk.co/)

Free for the next few months. Live & on-demand classes, on a range of topics with demonstrations of craft making related to that topic. Good for home and teachers.

[Teach Children ESL](http://www.teachchildrenesl.com/free-esl-games.html)

Lots of free downloadable games, worksheets, flashcards, and dice for parents to play with their children.

### Ages 2 - 8

[PBS](https://www.pbs.org/parents/)

Drop-down menu by age and content type: Math, Science, Literacy, Social-emotional, social skills, Character, and the Arts. Numerous fun games to play on a computer, phone or iPad.

### Elementary Grades

[All Kids Network](https://www.allkidsnetwork.com/)

All Kids Network has a vast array of printables for home: crafts, flashcards, various elementary versions of lined paper, and other resources.

[Crash Course Kids](https://www.youtube.com/user/crashcoursekids)

Educational video lessons on YouTube with a vast array of topics, such as space, mythology, literature, etc. Videos have closed captioning and run 3 – 5 minutes in length. Very child friendly to watch.

[Storyline Online](https://www.storylineonline.net/)

A wonderful website of picture-books read to children by well-known actors. Each book has a supplemental curriculum developed by elementary educators aimed at strengthening comprehension, verbal and written skills for ELLs. Each book lists the suggested grade levels and running time. It can be viewed on SchoolTube, YouTube or Vimeo.

### Ages 0 - 12

[Ranger Rick](https://www.rangerrick.org/)

Nature magazines: Ranger Rick (ages 7-12), Ranger Rick Jr. (ages 4-7) and Ranger Rick Cub (ages 0 – 4). Digital editions of their magazines are free to families for the next few months.

### Grades Pre-K - 5

[Read Conmigo](https://www.readconmigo.org/)

Read Conmigo provides free bilingual children’s books in Spanish and English for family reading. The eBooks include an activity to guide reading comprehension & vocabulary development. All eBooks are compatible with most Apple iOS, Amazon Kindle, & Android devices as well as laptop & desktop computers.

### Grades K - 5

[Lincoln Center At Home](https://www.lincolncenter.org/)

As a part of #LincolnCenterAtHome, the Lincoln Center Pop-Up Classroom brings you a daily lesson in creative art-making. Live broadcasts are every weekday morning at 10 AM EDT, a Lincoln Center Teaching Artist leads families and students in a workshop that uses simple materials found at home. Demonstrations are archived.

### Grades K - 8

[Games to Learn English](https://www.gamestolearnenglish.com/)

This is a free site for students to learn English online. There is a selection of games that students can use to practice learning English in a fun way. It is primarily aimed at ENL students, but young children may also benefit from them.

### Grades 6 - 12

[History.com](https://www.history.com/classroom)

An outstanding collection of topics, idea books, study guides, This Day in History, biographies, videos, appropriate for research projects.

### Grades K - 12

[Make Beliefs Comix](https://www.makebeliefscomix.com/)

Available to read and write in 13 languages. Empowers a student to express his/her ideas by creating comic strips. Has comic starters and printables. Choose from a diverse range of characters, objects, scenes, and emotions. Use talk and thought balloons. Free lesson plan. Also, read & write in free e-books.

[NY History](https://www.nyhistory.org/)

NYC Historical Society has numerous resources. Go in under Education – teachers - Curriculum Resources. Very good for students doing research papers.

### Ages 2 - College

[Khan Academy](https://www.khanacademy.org/)

Available in 22 languages. Free resources for students, teachers, and parents. Offers all content subjects, AP courses and SAT prep. Webinars, professional learning sessions, office hours to talk with a Kahn expert. Newly added Mastery Challenges offers more opportunities to review concepts students have already worked on.

### Pre-K - College

[NY Public Library](https://www.nypl.org/)

Using the free SimplyE reader app, available on the App Store or Google Play, one can borrow e-books for free. Excellent for research, primary source documents and photographs.

### Children - Adults

[Coloring Nature](https://www.coloringnature.org/)

Coloring Nature is for children & adults. There are more than 675 free printable coloring pages with new pages added weekly. Coloring categories include biomes, anatomy, animals, plants and more, plus some just for fun.

### All Grades

[Children's Library](https://es.childrenslibrary.org/)

International Children’s free Digital Library - an online library with hundreds of free books in Spanish for children. The text box enlarges the print.

#### Colorin Colorado

This bilingual site is a comprehensive resource for families of English Language Learners.

[NG Espanol](https://www.ngenespanol.com/)

National Geographic in Spanish Numerous high-quality videos and articles in Spanish.

[Photomath App](https://www.photomath.net/en/) - and Similar Math Apps

Take a picture of a Math problem with a cellphone and it will provide step by step solutions.

[Pura Vida Moms](https://www.puravidamoms.com/100-spanish-language-resources-for-bilingual-parents/)

100 Spanish language resources for bilingual parents.

[Reading Rockets](https://www.readingrockets.org/)

Sister-site to ColorinColorado. Numerous resources for parents to help their child at home with reading.

### All Ages

[Scholastic Learn at Home](https://classroommagazines.scholastic.com/support/learnathome/resources-for-families.html)

Free resources of activities to do with children at home.

[Teachers First](https://www.teachersfirst.com/par-esl.cfm)

Numerous resources for parents of ENL students to use at home; some are good to reinforce language skills.

### Ungraded

[LibriVox](https://www.librivox.org/)

LibriVox makes books available, for free, in audio format. The catalog is sortable by author, title, genre/subject, and language. Over 1,000 books are available in numerous languages.

[Simple English Wikipedia](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simple_English_Wikipedia)

For ENL students doing research papers at home, Simple English Wikipedia uses shorter sentences, easier words, and grammar than the regular English Wikipedia. Translates into over 50 languages.

[The American Museum of Natural History](https://www.amnh.org/)

The American Museum of Natural History – their Science website for kids is called Ology. This has games, stories, hands-on activities and videos under a number of topics. Short stories have a quiz at the end. Very user friendly.

[The Smithsonian Museum](https://www.si.edu/)

The Smithsonian Museum in Washington, DC has a number of pictures of their individual museums. Click on any one of those museums for specifics: the opening page has numerous photos. Click on the exhibitions link and then “learn more” which gives in-depth information about specific topics and short, informational, easy to read paragraphs.

## Students with Disabilities/Special Education Supports

### Birth Through College

[IncludeNY](https://www.includeny.org/)

NYC based organization that provides free services. The website is in Spanish and English; it provides excellent support for parents of students with disabilities. Free phone-in helpline delivers in-depth, 1;1 assistance with special education or disability-related issues to parents, young adults, and professionals. Easy website to navigate.

### All Ages

[LDOnline](https://www.ldonline.org/)

Long established website. Very comprehensive resources and sub-links on understanding and supporting individuals with learning disabilities. [http://www.ldonline.org/resources%20new/c698](http://www.ldonline.org/resources new/c698) (parent resources)

## Zoos and Outdoor Webcams

### All Grades/Ages

#### Eagle and Hawks Nests

Free webcams of [Eagle](https://www.eagles.org/) and [Hawks](https://hdontap.com/) nests across the USA. They provide high-definition live webcams of the Bald Eagle and Hawks nesting. Wonderful opportunity to watch eggs hatch; engages the whole family in language discussions.

[Jenkinson's Aquarium Penguin Cam](https://jenkinsons.com/aquarium/penguin-cam/)

View livestream of penguins.

[Seven Zoos and Aquariums](https://ourcommunitynow.com/attractions/7-zoos-and-aquariums-that-let-you-watch-live-videos-of-their-animals-for-free)

Live webcams. Wonderful variety across the USA. Lots of home conversations can be generated from this.

[The Bronx Zoo](https://bronxzoo.com/the-zoo)

Videos of animals at the zoo with narration. Links to other NYC zoos.

[The Duke Farms Eagle Cam](https://www.dukefarms.org/making-an-impact/eagle-cam/)

View livestream of newly hatched eaglets in their nest.

[The San Diego Zoo](https://zoo.sandiegozoo.org/live-cams)

Live webcams of owls, condors, tigers, elephants, butterflies, giraffes, koala bears, apes, panda bears, polar bears, baboons, and penguins. Wait several seconds for it to turn on.

[The Smithsonian's National Zoo and Conservation Biology Institute](https://nationalzoo.si.edu/webcams)

The National Zoo has live webcams of its naked mole rats, panda bears, lions, and elephants. Wait for several seconds for it to turn on.
