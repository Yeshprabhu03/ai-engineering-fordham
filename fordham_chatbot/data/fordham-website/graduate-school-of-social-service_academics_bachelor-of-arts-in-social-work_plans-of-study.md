# B.A.S.W. Plans of Study

![Fordham gss banner basw](/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/Fordham_GSS_Banner___BASW.png)


The Bachelor of Arts in Social Work (B.A.S.W.) major at Fordham is a 31-credit program. All students are expected to complete the major in two years.

The major consists of:

- Eight required classes taken in sequence.
- A year-long, 450-hour Fieldwork Practicum and an Integrative Seminar integrating classroom knowledge with your experiences in the field.

## Coursework

The coursework is spread across four semesters so you can:

- Start Semester 1 in fall or spring.
- Start Semester 2 in summer or fall.

## Field Instruction

[Fieldwork](/graduate-school-of-social-service/academics/bachelor-of-arts-in-social-work/field-instruction/) takes place in the second year. The 450-hour course consists of:

- 420-hour practicum in generalist social work practice in an agency setting. (You can work either 21 or 14 hours per week.)
- 30 hours of the Integrative Seminar that meets every other week.
