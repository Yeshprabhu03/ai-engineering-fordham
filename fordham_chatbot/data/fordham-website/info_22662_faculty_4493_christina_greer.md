Christina Greer is an Associate Professor of Political Science at Fordham University, Lincoln Center (Manhattan) campus. Her research and teaching focus on American politics, Black ethnic politics, campaigns and elections, and public opinion. She is the author of

[Black Ethnics: Race, Immigration, and the Pursuit of the American Dream](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/black-ethnics-9780199989317?q=black%20ethnics&lang=en&cc=us),[How to Build a Democracy from Fannie Lou Hamer and Barbara Jordan to Stacey Abrams](https://www.cambridge.org/core/elements/abs/how-to-build-a-democracy/F34AB82F40A6FFD835D39C8AF46DA530), and co-editor of[Black Politics in Transition: Immigration, Suburbanization, and Gentrification](https://www.routledge.com/Black-Politics-in-Transition-Immigration-Suburbanization-and-Gentrification/WattsSmith-Greer/p/book/9781138058507?srsltid=AfmBOoqUT-m193w_SMVwLUctKIFI2gFw3eWhTmBs3EOoIF-Y0U0nQOpW). Greer writes a weekly column for The Amsterdam News, is a frequent political commentator on several media outlets, and is the co-host of FAQ-NYC.Her research interests also include mayors and public policy in urban centers. Her previous work has compared criminal activity and political responses in Boston and Baltimore. Prof. Greer received her B.A. from Tufts University and her M.A., M.Phil., and Ph.D. in Political Science from Columbia University.
