# J.D. Curriculum Guide

## Your interests, your future.


**At Fordham Law, legal education is individualized, supportive, and compelling. **You will experience the rigors of the classroom and can pursue the excitement of hands-on fieldwork in a wide range of legal specialty areas.

**You may select from some 200 elective courses, helping you discover your legal interests.** Gain firsthand experience through
experiential education in Fordham Law’s
[clinics](/school-of-law/experiential-education/clinics/) and
[J.D. externships](/school-of-law/experiential-education/jd-externships/).
[Class schedules](/school-of-law/academics/registrars-office/registration-information/class-schedules/) for recent and upcoming semesters give you a sense of the breadth and depth you’ll find at Fordham Law.

As you chart your path through the School’s curriculum, you will take Corporations, Professional Responsibility, and courses that satisfy the Fordham Law’s skills and
[legal writing](/school-of-law/academics/curriculum/jd-curriculum/legal-writing-program/) requirements, as detailed in our
[Academic Regulations](/school-of-law/academics/registrars-office/academic-regulations/).

**If you want to focus on a particular field of law, explore our
J.D. concentrations. **You can sharpen your knowledge in a group of classes associated with specialized areas of legal practice. With a concentration, you will signal to employers that you’re prepared for focused legal work when you graduate.
