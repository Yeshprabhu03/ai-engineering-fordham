[[email protected]](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#b9cad5d0d7d8cbcddc88f9dfd6cbddd1d8d497dcddcc)Sofia Linarte is the Director of Engagement and Counseling at Fordham Law. Sofia works closely with law students, faculty, and the broader legal community to identify and support opportunities that connect students with enriching, experiential learning in the service of others. Sofia also provides students with individualized public interest career counseling and supports the exceptional work of PIRC's student-led organizations.

Prior to joining PIRC, Sofia was the Managing Attorney at Catholic Charities NYC’s Unaccompanied Children’s Program (“UCP”) where she led a team of 50 attorneys, case managers, and other legal professionals in annually helping thousands of young immigrants who were facing family separation, federal government detention, and deportation by providing pro bono know-your-rights presentations, legal screenings, and legal representation. Outside the courtroom, UCP welcomed recently-arrived young immigrants with educational and integration opportunities, social services, and community-building experiences. Sofia has practiced across various state and federal settings, and has extensive expertise in non-profit legal program management and in representing survivors of complex trauma.

Sofia earned her B.A. at Rice University and her J.D. at Fordham University School of Law. During law school, Sofia was a Stein Scholar, a Feerick Fellow, a New York Women’s Bar Association Foundation Fellow, served as president of two PIRC student organizations, and earned the Archibald R. Murray Public Service Award summa cum laude.

Sofia is admitted to practice law in New York State and the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit.
