While in law school, she interned for the Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia and the Alaska Public Defender. She also served as a Research Assistant for the International Criminal Courtâ€™s Legal Tools Project. After law school, Michelle taught English in Tbilisi, was a research fellow at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum, and obtained her PhD in legal history from the University of Colorado with a dissertation titled *The Extermination of Peaceful Soviet Citizens: Aron Trainin and International Law*. Michelle received her MLIS degree from the University of Arizona in 2018, where she served as a Law Library Fellow.
