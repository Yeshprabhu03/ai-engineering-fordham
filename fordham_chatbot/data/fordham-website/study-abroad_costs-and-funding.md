# Costs and Funding

![Students stand in front of temple](/media/home/departments-centers-and-offices/study-abroad/Karin-1.png)

Students who study abroad for one semester or more receive a bill from Fordham University's Office of Student Accounts, which in most cases covers the cost of tuition, housing, orientation services, and insurance.

"Out of Pocket" expenses, such as transportation, airfare, books, meals, visa fees, and entertainment are generally not included in this bill.

Students who opt to enroll in a short-term study abroad program that is not administered by Fordham University pay all fees directly to their host institutions except the International Insurance Fee.

Tuition: Tuition is paid directly to host institution

Housing: Housing is paid directly to host institution

Other Fees: A $85 Fordham Study Abroad Application Fee will be charged to participants

International Insurance Fee ($90 per month*)

**Please note insurance is billed based on program start and end dates, not length of time. Programs that are less than one month in length, but start and end in the same month will incur a $90 insurance fee. Programs that start and end in different months will be billed $90 for each month the program takes place in.*
