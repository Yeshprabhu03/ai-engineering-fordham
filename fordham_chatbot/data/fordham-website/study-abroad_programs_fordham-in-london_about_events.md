This day-long symposium at Fordham London brings together professionals from a variety of disciplines to address the pervasive challenge posed by online abuse and threats. This event is dedicated both to raising awareness of the forms and effects of online abuse and to developing robust, practical solutions to this growing problem.

The program includes:

- Quantitative data and detection strategies presented by
[Signify](https://www.signify.ai/)(online abuse in sports). - An academic panel featuring Fordham London faculty tackling the difficult intersection of free speech and online harm.
- Testimony from a survivor of online abuse and advocacy insights from
[Jesuit Refugee Service UK](https://www.jrsuk.net/).

We will conclude with a high-profile, joint event with the British American Project - UK, uniting US and UK leaders in a policy-focused discussion on accountability, legislation, and collective action designed to protect vulnerable communities online.

In collaboration with Fordham University's School of Professional and Continuing Studies and their College at 60 program, we are delighted to invite you to attend the inaugural
**College at 60 London program**,**The History of British Government and Politics**, which will be**hosted and delivered by Fordham University London on May 7-17, 2026**.This study abroad short course is open to all alumni in addition to students from Fordham's College at 60 program. The program will be delivered at Fordham University's London campus.The course will explore the unique aspects of British political history and life in Britain today. Professor John Annette, former President and Vice-Chancellor of Richmond University and a specialist in political science, will be your primary lecturer, providing commentary on topics ranging from the history of the monarchy to the role of Parliament. He will be joined by other members of the Fordham London faculty who will lecture on their areas of expertise, including Brexit and contemporary London.

Included in the cost of the program is housing at Fordham London's Clerkenwell student accommodation, daily breakfasts, special receptions and meals, lectures from faculty, and excursions related to the course. For further details and a full list of what's included, please visit[fordham.edu/](http://fordham.edu/collegeat60london).collegeat60london
