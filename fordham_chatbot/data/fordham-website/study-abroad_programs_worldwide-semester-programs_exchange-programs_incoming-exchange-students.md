# Incoming Exchange Students

#### Welcome incoming exchange students!

![Building - SM](https://www.fordham.edu/media/review/content-assets/migrated/images/fu_100908_0232.nef__SM.jpg)

Fordham University has partnered with numerous universities across the globe, and through these partnerships, international students have the opportunity to study at Fordham University for one semester or one academic year (August to May). To participate in the exchange program, please confirm that your home university is currently a [partner](http://internationalprograms.fordham.edu/index.cfm?Program_Type_ID=1&Program_Name=exchange&pt=%7F&pi=%7F&pc=%7F&pr=%7F&FuseAction=Programs.SearchResults&SimpleSearch=1) with Fordham University for undergraduate exchange.

If your home university is **not** currently a partner with Fordham University, please visit the Office of Admissions' page regarding [visiting international students](https://www.fordham.edu/undergraduate-admission/international-students/visiting-international-students/).

**(Prospective incoming exchange students must be officially nominated by their home university before gaining access to the application)**

New York City is made up of five boroughs: Manhattan, the Bronx, Queens, Brooklyn, and Staten Island. Fordham University has two campuses in New York City: Rose Hill (the Bronx), Lincoln Center (Manhattan). The

[Ram Van](/student-life/department-of-transportation/ram-van-shuttle/)shuttle is an inter-campus van service that is available to all Fordham students. Both campuses share most academic departments, but each specializes in a couple fields.The

**Rose Hill**campus is the original campus of Fordham University. It is a traditional college campus, with green lawns, trees, large residence halls, sports facilities, and outdoor American football and baseball stadiums. The campus has the benefits of being located next to the NY Botanical Garden, the Bronx Zoo, and famed Arthur Avenue. The Rose Hill campus is well connected to transit, with a 20 minute Metro North train ride to Grand Central Station, Citi Bike stations, multiple bus lines, and subway station within walking distance. Academically, Rose Hill host a broader array of sciences majors.The

**Lincoln Center**campus is located in Midtown Manhattan, immediately next the the world renowned Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts. It is an urban campus, occupying a single city block, with tall academic and residential buildings encompassing an outdoor plaza. Also well connected to transit, Lincoln Center is has subway and bus connections to the Grand Central and Penn Stations. In addition the the Fordham-wide majors, Lincoln Center hosts the Theatre and Dance departments. -

[undergraduate degree programs](/undergraduate-admission/majors-and-minors/)available as well as browse the[course catalog](https://reg-prod.ec.fordham.edu/StudentRegistrationSsb/ssb/term/termSelection?mode=search)and view[course descriptions](https://bulletin.fordham.edu/courses/). In the course catalog, undergraduate courses has a 4-digit course number between 1000 and 4999. Any lower or higher numbers are graduate-level and not available to exchange students. Your home university transcripts will be reviewed to assess prerequisites for your Fordham courses. You will be allowed to add or drop courses during the first week of classes. In order to change your classes, you must reach out the the International and Study Abroad Programs office with your request. Some subjects are reserved for degree-seeking students only.*Note: Course selection and availability is not guaranteed for exchange students.*You must register for a regular full-time course load which is the equivalent of

**15-18 credits**upon your arrival at Fordham University.**All incoming exchange students will be enrolled in a mandatory 3 credit exchange course during their first semester.****Intercultural Exchange**ILC 1271 - (3 credits)*This course is intended for exchange students and introduces basic concepts, skills, and strategies to identify and manage cultural and academic expectations in the US. The course is designed to support students familiarize with the contemporary American culture, to help them understand and appreciate it, and to help them feel “at home” in NYC. Each student, as a representative of his/her own culture, will have a “voice” in this class, since the main intention is to encourage a cultural dialog (vs a cultural clash). Through weekly readings, ethnographic observations, and practical discussions, students will learn the importance of preconceptions, context, roles and power in intercultural communication. Students will identify opportunities and challenges that arise from cultural differences and learn ways to appropriately address them in both academic and social settings.* -

Once you have been officially nominated by your home institution, you will receive an email notification from Fordham University indicating an application has been created on your behalf. In the email you will find your login credentials: a username (your personal email address) and a temporary password. Students

**must**select the option "Non-Fordham" login[link](https://fordham-ip.terradotta.com/index.cfm?FuseAction=Security.Login)to proceed to create your own password. Once the login credentials are verified, you will gain access to your Fordham Study Abroad Portal and be prompted to complete additional personal details. Your portal homepage will have application instructions and the requirements to be completed. -
All students who do not have a United States passport will be required to apply for a J-1 Visitor Visa. More information about the visa process and application will be made available during the application process.

As part of the J-1 visa

[requirements](http://j1visa.state.gov/sponsors/how-to-administer-a-program/)(click on "Insurance"), you must have sufficient medical insurance coverage. You will need to provide proof of your medical insurance coverage upon your arrival to Fordham University in order to waive the[Fordham insurance](/academics/academic-resources/international-services/students/current-students/health-insurance-information/)that will be charged to your student account. If you do not waive the Fordham Insurance plan by the deadline, you will be charged for the insurance and required to pay the charge prior to the release of your Fordham transcript.In order to receive the Certificate of Eligibility (DS-2019) needed to obtain a visa, international students must provide evidence that they have financial support to cover at least $10,000 for the living expenses for each semester/term they will attend Fordham University. All exchange students must complete the Certificate of Financial Eligibility (AFCOE) form which will be available in your study abroad portal.

**Fordham University's exchange program does not authorize internships for students on J-1 visas.** -
