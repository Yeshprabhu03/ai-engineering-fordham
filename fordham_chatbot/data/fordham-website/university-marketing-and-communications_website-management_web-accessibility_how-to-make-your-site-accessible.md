# How to Make Your Site Accessible

We use software called [SiteImprove](https://siteimprove.com/) to help us monitor our pages' accessibility and identify issues. The Online Communications team will grant access to SiteImprove so your team can receive weekly reports on how your pages are performing with accessibility in mind. Customized dashboards show issues in order of priority, and will also show you progress as they are resolved.

[Siteimprove Introduction to Web Accessibility Video](https://www.siteimprove.com/on-demand-webinars/an-introduction-to-web-accessibility/)

[Siteimprove Onboarding: Introduction and Accessibility](https://learninghub.siteimprove.com/siteimprove-onboarding-part-1-introduction-and-accessibility)

Please let us know who should have access to Siteimprove and we will create their accounts.

Learn more about making your [web pages accessible](https://www.siteimprove.com/blog/no-more-excuses--there-is-now-a-u.s.-deadline-for-becoming-accessible/) and see SiteImprove's [web accessibility checklist](https://hello.siteimprove.com/en/whitepaper/accessible-blogging-checklist/download).

Contact the [Online Engagement team](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#790e1c1b0a100d1c1f1c1c1d1b181a12391f160b1d111814571c1d0c) for access to Siteimprove to review Accessibility reports.
